A bowl of butternut squash soup

A smooth, creamy soup made from puréed butternut squash, often enhanced with ingredients like onions, carrots, broth, and spices, and served with toppings such as croutons, fresh herbs, or a swirl of cream.

ingredient

  • 1 ½ tablespoons butter
  • ½ onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • ½ butternut squash - peeled, seeded, and cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • ½ cube chicken bouillon
  • 1 pinch ground cumin
  • 1 pinch ground allspice
  • salt and ground black pepper to taste

instructions

  1. Step 1

    2. Melt butter in a large pot over medium heat. Add onion, garlic, and thyme and sauté until onion has softened, about 5 minutes. Add squash and chicken broth; bring to a simmer, then cook until squash is tender, 10 to 15 minutes. Crumble bouillon into the soup. Season with cumin, allspice, salt, and pepper, then remove from heat.

  2. Step 2

    3. Working in batches, pour soup into a blender, filling no more than halfway. Hold the lid in place with a kitchen towel, then carefully start the blender using a few quick pulses to get the soup moving before leaving it on to purée. Pour soup into a serving bowl. Alternately, you can use a stick blender and purée the soup in the pot.
